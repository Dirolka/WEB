Nikola Tesla

1856–1943 • Engineer, physicist, and inventor

About Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856–1943) was a Serbian‑American inventor and engineer who made foundational contributions to electrical engineering and alternating‑current (AC) systems. He developed the induction motor and polyphase systems, enabling efficient power transmission over long distances. Tesla’s ideas in radio, wireless power, and high‑frequency technology had a profound impact on science and industry in the 20th century.

Key events

  1. 1856 — Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (now Croatia).
  2. 1884 — Moved to the United States and began working in New York.
  3. 1888 — Presented the AC system and the induction motor.
  4. 1891 — Invented the Tesla transformer (Tesla coil).
  5. 1895–1896 — Work in radio communication and high‑frequency currents.
  6. 1943 — Died in New York; his legacy greatly influenced power engineering.

Inventions and contributions

  • Polyphase systems and AC power transmission.
  • Induction motor and transformers.
  • Tesla coil and high‑frequency research.
  • Contributions to radio and wireless technologies.
  • Ideas on wireless power transfer.
